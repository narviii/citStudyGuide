# Canadian historic figures


### Sidney Altman
A Nobel Prize-winning scientist.
### Denys Arcand 
Filmmaker whose films have been popular in Quebec and across the country, and have won international awards. 
### Kenojuak Ashevak
Pioneer of the modern Inuit art with etchings, prints and soapstone sculptures.
### Donovan Bailey
A world record sprinter and double Olympic gold medallist after the Olympic Summer Games in 1996. 
### Robert Baldwin
One of reformers who worked towards responsible government after the Upper and Lower Canada were united as the Province of Canada in 1840. 
### Jim Balsillie — of Research in Motion (RIM)
A wireless communications company known for its most famous invention: the **BlackBerry**.
### Alexander Graham Bell
British inventor who hit on the idea of the telephone at his summer house in Canada.
### Captain Billy Bishop
Earned the Victoria Cross in the Royal Flying Corps during the First World War, and was later an honorary Air Marshal of the Royal Canadian Air Force.
### Joseph-Armand Bombardier
Inventor of the snowmobile, a light-weight winter vehicle.
### Sir Robert Borden 
Prime minister who gave women the right to vote in federal elections
### Joseph Brant 
Led thousands of Loyalist Mohawk Indians into Canada after the American Revolution happened.
### Major-General Sir Isaac Brock
One of the leaders of the British army during the War of 1812. He captured Detroit but was killed while defending against an American attack at Queenston Heights, near Niagara Falls, a battle the Americans lost. 
### Bertram Brockhouse 
A Nobel Prize-winning scientist.
### John Buchan
The 1st Baron Tweedsmuir and a popular Governor General of Canada (1935–40). Immigrant groups, he said, “should retain their individuality and each make its contribution to the national character.” Each could learn “from the other, and … while they cherish their own special loyalties and traditions, they cherish not less that new loyalty and tradition which springs from their union.”
### John Cabot
The first to draw a map of Canada’s East Coast, setting foot on Newfoundland or Cape Breton Island in 1497 and claiming the New Founde Land for England. 
### Emily Carr
Painter was closely associated (though never an official member) with the Group of Seven founded in 1920. 
The Group of Seven developed a style of painting to capture the rugged wilderness landscapes. In the visual arts, Canada is historically best known for its work.
### Jacques Cartier
Between 1534 and 1542, Cartier made three voyages across the Atlantic, claiming the land for King Francis I of France. He was the first European to explore the St. Lawrence River and to set eyes on present-day Québec City and Montreal.
Cartier heard two captured guides speak the Iroquoian word kanata, meaning “village.” By the 1550s, the name of Canada began appearing on maps.
### Sir George-Étienne Cartier
Father of Confederation, its key architect from Quebec, Cartier led Quebec into Confederation and helped negotiate the entry of the Northwest Territories, Manitoba and British Columbia into Canada.
Thérèse Casgrain
Leader in the fight for women's right to vote in Quebec.
### Sir Arthur Currie
“Canada’s greatest soldier”, commander of Canadian Corps during the World War I. 
### Robertson Davies
Writer.
### Gabriel Dumont
The Métis’ greatest military leader, a fried and ally of Lois Riel.
### Lieutenant Alexander Roberts Dunn
The first Canadian to be awarded the Victoria Cross, served in the
British Army in the Crimean War.
### Lord Durham
An English reformer sent to report on the rebellions of 1837-1838 who recommended that Upper and Lower Canada be merged and given responsible government. This meant that the ministers of the Crown must have the support of a majority of the elected representatives in order to govern.
Controversially, Lord Durham also said that the quickest way for the Canadiens to achieve progress was to assimilate into English-speaking Protestant culture. This recommendation demonstrated a complete lack of understanding of French Canadians, who sought to uphold the distinct identity of French Canada.
### Phil Edwards
Canadian track and field champion won bronze medals for Canada in the 1928, 1932 and 1936 Olympics, then graduated from McGill University Medical School.
He served as a captain in the Canadian Army during the Second World War and, as a Montreal doctor, became an expert in tropical diseases.
### Atom Egoyan
Filmmaker. 
### Lord Elgin
The governor of United Canada who introduced its responsible government in 1848–49.
### Matthew Evans
Inventor who together with Henry Woodward invented the first electric light bulb and later sold the patent to Thomas Edison.
### Reginald Fessenden
Contributor to the invention of radio who sent the first wireless
voice message in the world.
### Sir Sandford Fleming
Inventor of the worldwide system of standard time zones.
### Sir Louis-Hippolyte La Fontaine
The first leader of a responsible government in the Canadas (Premier of the Province of Canda). He was a champion of democracy and French language rights and one of reformers who worked towards responsible government after the Upper and Lower Canada were united as the Province of Canada in 1840. 
### Terry Fox
A British Columbian who lost his right leg to cancer at the age of 18
began a cross-country run, the “Marathon of Hope,” in 1980 to raise money for cancer research. He became a hero to Canadians. While he did not finish the run and ultimately lost his battle with cancer, his legacy continues through yearly fundraising events in his name. 
### Count Frontenac
One of the leaders of the French colonization in New France.
### Lieutenant Robert Hampton Gray
A navy pilot killed while bombing and sinking a Japanese warship in August 1945, a few days before the end of the Second World War, the last Canadian to receive the Victoria Cross to date.
### Wayne Gretzky
One of the greatest hockey players of all time played for the Edmonton Oilers from 1979 to 1988.
### Lord Grey
The Governor General who donated the Grey Cup for the championship of professional teams in the Canadian Football League (CFL). 
### Able Seaman William Hall
The first black man to be awarded the Victoria Cross for his role in the Siege of Lucknow during the Indian Rebellion of 1857.
### Rick Hansen
A British Columbian circled the globe in a wheelchair in 1985 to raise funds for spinal cord research.
### Louis-Philippe Hébert
Quebec’s celebrated sculptor of historical figures. 
### Louis Hémon
Writer.
### Gerhard Herzberg
A refugee from Nazi Germany and a Nobel Prize-winning scientist.
### Dr. John A. Hopps
Inventor of the first cardiac pacemaker, used today to save the lives of people with heart disorders.
### Joseph Howe
Reformer who worked towards responsible government in Nova Scotia. Thanks to his efforts, Nova Scotia became the first British North American colony to attain full responsible government 1847–48. 
### Harold Innis
Political economist and pioneer in communication studies.
### Norman Jewison
Filmmaker.
### Pauline Johnson

### Joy Kogawa
Writer.

### Margaret Laurence
Writer.

### Bishop Laval
One of the leaders of the French colonization in New France.
